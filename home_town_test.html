Explore Mandalay

Discover the cultural heart of Myanmar through its ancient capitals, vibrant markets, and rich traditions.

The Founding of a Capital

Founded in 1857 by King Mindon, Mandalay was the last royal capital of Burma before the British annexation. It was built to fulfill a prophecy and stands as a testament to the rich cultural and religious heritage of the nation. The city layout was meticulously planned at the foot of Mandalay Hill, with the Royal Palace at its center, surrounded by a perfect square of city walls and a moat.

The construction of the city was a monumental undertaking, involving thousands of workers and elephants. Despite the turbulence of history, including World War II destruction and subsequent fires, the spirit of the royal capital endures. Today, it remains the cultural heartbeat of the country, where ancient traditions of Buddhism, arts, and crafts continue to thrive amidst the changing tides of the modern world.

Did You Know?

Mandalay was the last royal capital of Burma before British colonial rule. The city layout is based on a grid system, centered around the majestic Mandalay Palace, a 413-hectare walled fortress surrounded by a moat.
